// SUBJECT: RANDOM THOUGHTS

I think most people are rehearsing. Smiles are rehearsed. Opinions are rehearsed. Even outrage feels scripted. We call it personality, but it is mostly adaptation, tone adjustment, posture correction, vocabulary calibration.

I do it too.

There is a version of me that laughs louder. A version that speaks softer. A version that pretends not to think too much. None of them are lies, but none of them are whole.

Sometimes I wonder if authenticity is just another aesthetic, another role you perform when you want to feel brave. Even Eli is suspicious.

If I am aware of the mask, is that awareness real, or is this just a deeper layer of performance pretending to be depth?

People say “be yourself” like there is a stable object underneath. What if there isn’t. What if the self is just a negotiation between environment and fear.

If that’s true, then identity is fluid. Not poetic fluid. Mechanical fluid. Input changes. Output changes.

And somewhere in the switching, something watches.

I used to think that watcher was the real me.

Now I think that watcher is Eli.

Not a truer self. Not a deeper one.

Just the version that refuses to clap when the performance ends.

The one that stays seated in the dark after everyone else goes home.

Watching.
